# Client Journey

This document describes the client experience from first contact through renewal.

It maps business stages to the artifacts and approvals that make the engagement predictable.

How To Use:

Read this file when designing onboarding, managing expectations, or mapping client touchpoints to internal work.

## Journey Stages

1. Lead or referral arrives.
2. Discovery call confirms fit.
3. Client interview gathers structured input.
4. Marketing brief defines the problem and goals.
5. Strategy artifacts shape the engagement.
6. Production artifacts are created and reviewed.
7. Final delivery and handoff are completed.
8. Renewal, expansion, or offboarding is decided.

## Key Touchpoints

- Discovery
- Qualification
- Onboarding
- Approval
- Delivery
- Revision handling
- Renewal

## Journey Rules

- The client should always know the next approval point.
- Each stage should have a clear owner and expected output.
- The journey should mirror the canonical artifact flow in [README](../README.md).
