# Business Model

This document defines the commercial operating model for the AI-First Marketing Agency.

It describes what the agency sells, how work is packaged, and which artifacts are produced for each engagement.

How To Use:

Read this file before designing offers, scopes, or delivery rules. Use it together with [Pricing](pricing.md) and [Service Catalog](service_catalog.md) when defining a client-facing engagement.

## Purpose

- Define the agency as a structured, artifact-driven service business.
- Keep business decisions separate from role definitions and execution details.
- Make the delivery model predictable for both humans and agents.

## Core Model

- Start with a client need or opportunity.
- Qualify the engagement through discovery.
- Produce a [Client Interview](../schemas/client_interview.schema.json), then a [Marketing Brief](../schemas/marketing_brief.schema.json).
- Move into strategy, production, review, and delivery through the canonical artifact flow documented in [README](../README.md) and [Project](../PROJECT.md).

## Business Rules

- One primary owner per artifact.
- Every paid engagement must have a defined scope and approval path.
- Every deliverable must map to a named artifact or documented output.
- Business docs should stay aligned with [Agency](../agency/DESCRIPTION.md), [Marketing](../marketing/DESCRIPTION.md), and [Schemas](../schemas/DESCRIPTION.md).

## Key Outputs

- Offer definition
- Scope boundaries
- Delivery model
- Pricing model
- Client handoff expectations
